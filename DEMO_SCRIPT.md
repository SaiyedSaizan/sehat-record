# Demo Script

## 3-minute founder walkthrough

1. Open the landing page.
Say: "Sehat Record turns scattered paper prescriptions into one usable health record for families and a time-saving summary for doctors."

2. Click into demo mode.
Say: "We have three realistic patient stories seeded for demo day: chronic care, elderly-parent management, and acute illness."

3. Open Rajesh Sharma.
Say: "This is the kind of patient who usually brings multiple old slips from different clinics. Instead of reading each paper manually, the dashboard immediately shows conditions, current medicines, repeated prescriptions, and searchable visit history."

4. Use the search bar.
Search for `Telmisartan` or `Raghav`.
Say: "Doctors or family members can search by medicine, doctor, condition, or date instead of hunting through files."

5. Open Doctor Summary.
Say: "This is the time-saving clinician view. Past 5 visits, active medicines, prior tests, and basic duplicate-risk flags are in one place. It helps the doctor understand continuity faster without claiming any diagnostic intelligence."

6. Open a prescription detail page.
Say: "Patients often do not understand why each medicine was written. Here the same prescription is translated into simpler language with safer wording like 'this appears to be' and 'please confirm with your doctor or pharmacist.'"

7. Open Timeline.
Say: "The timeline makes fragmented care understandable across clinics. You can see visits, medicines started, and tests advised in chronological order."

8. Go to Upload Prescription.
Say: "For the MVP we use a mock extraction pipeline, but the flow is real. A user uploads a prescription, reviews extracted details, corrects medicine names or dosage, and saves it into the same longitudinal record."

9. Open the review screen and save.
Say: "Manual correction is not a workaround. In India, it is part of a trustworthy workflow because prescriptions are often handwritten or noisy."

10. Close with the value proposition.
Say: "Patients get ownership and clarity. Doctors get speed. Sehat Record starts with the messy reality of paper and turns it into a usable health timeline."
